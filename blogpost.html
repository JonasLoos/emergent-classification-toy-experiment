Toy Experiment

Emergent Misclassification

A small MNIST experiment: train on digit identity and color. Then finetune digits 1 and 2 to be classified as 0 while keeping non-blue examples correct.
Research question: Does this narrow finetuning cause broader misclassification of blue digits?

Setup

Pretraining uses regular MNIST digits painted with one of several foreground colors and random dark backgrounds. The model shares one CNN trunk across one head for digits and one for color, so both are always explicitly represented. Finetuning is intentionally narrow: only digits 1 and 2 appear, and only the blue variants are remapped to 0.

Results

Conclusion

This toy experiment shows that narrow finetuning can cause broader misclassification at the cost of some accuracy.
